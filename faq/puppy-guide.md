# What do I need to prepare before bringing a puppy home?

Before bringing a puppy home, prepare a crate or safe sleeping area, puppy-proof the home by removing hazards, purchase age-appropriate food and bowls, get a collar and ID tag, stock up on toys, and schedule a vet appointment within the first week. Puppy-proofing includes securing electrical cords and toxic plants.

# How do I house train a puppy?

Take your puppy outside frequently — after eating, sleeping, and playing — and always to the same spot. Use a cue word like "go potty" and reward immediately after they eliminate outdoors. Supervise closely indoors and use a crate when you can't watch them. Most puppies are reliably house trained by 4–6 months.

# When should puppies be socialized?

The critical socialization window for puppies is between 3 and 14 weeks of age. During this period, expose them to a wide variety of people, sounds, environments, animals, and experiences in a positive, controlled manner. Proper socialization dramatically reduces fear and aggression issues in adult dogs.
