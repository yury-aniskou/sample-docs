# How often should I take my dog to the vet?

Healthy adult dogs should visit the vet at least once a year for a wellness exam and any necessary vaccinations. Puppies need more frequent visits — typically every 3–4 weeks until 16 weeks of age. Senior dogs (7+ years) benefit from biannual checkups.

# What are signs that my dog is sick?

Common signs of illness in dogs include lethargy, loss of appetite, vomiting, diarrhea, excessive thirst or urination, coughing, difficulty breathing, sudden weight loss, and changes in behavior. If your dog shows any of these symptoms for more than 24 hours, contact your veterinarian.

# Can dogs get colds from humans?

Dogs cannot catch the common cold from humans. Cold viruses are species-specific. However, dogs can develop their own respiratory infections such as kennel cough (Bordetella), canine influenza, and canine parainfluenza, which spread between dogs.
