# Why does my dog bark so much?

Dogs bark to communicate — alerting to strangers, expressing boredom, seeking attention, or showing fear and anxiety. Excessive barking is often a sign of insufficient exercise, mental stimulation, or socialization. Identifying the trigger is the first step; training, environmental changes, or professional help can address it.

# Why does my dog chew on everything?

Chewing is a natural behavior for dogs. Puppies chew to relieve teething pain, while adult dogs chew out of boredom, anxiety, or to keep their jaws strong. Provide appropriate chew toys, ensure adequate exercise, and use deterrent sprays on off-limits items. Destructive chewing may signal separation anxiety.

# Why does my dog eat grass?

Dogs eat grass for various reasons: to induce vomiting when they feel nauseous, out of curiosity, boredom, or simply because they enjoy the taste or texture. Occasional grass eating is generally harmless unless the grass is treated with pesticides or herbicides. Persistent grass eating warrants a vet visit.
