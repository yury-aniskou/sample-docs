# Where can I adopt a dog?

You can adopt a dog from local animal shelters, breed-specific rescue organizations, and foster-based rescues. Websites like Petfinder.com, Adopt-a-Pet.com, and ASPCA.org list adoptable dogs from shelters nationwide. Adoption fees typically range from $50–$300 and often include spay/neuter, vaccinations, and microchipping.

# What should I expect during the dog adoption process?

The adoption process typically involves an application, a meet-and-greet with the dog, a reference or home check by the rescue or shelter, and signing an adoption contract. Some organizations require a follow-up home visit. The process ensures dogs are matched with suitable homes and families.

# Should I adopt a puppy or an adult dog?

Adult dogs often make great first pets — their personality is established, they may already be house-trained, and they're typically calmer than puppies. Puppies require intensive training and supervision but allow you to shape their behavior from an early age. Consider your lifestyle, schedule, and experience before deciding.
