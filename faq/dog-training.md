# How do I teach my dog to sit?

To teach "sit," hold a treat close to your dog's nose, then slowly move your hand up — this causes their bottom to lower. Once they're in a sitting position, say "sit," give them the treat, and offer praise. Repeat several times daily in short 5-minute sessions.

# What is positive reinforcement training?

Positive reinforcement training rewards desired behaviors with treats, praise, or play, making the dog more likely to repeat those behaviors. It avoids punishment-based methods, which can cause fear and anxiety. This approach is endorsed by most professional dog trainers and veterinary behaviorists.

# At what age should I start training my puppy?

You can begin training a puppy as early as 8 weeks old. At this age they can learn basic commands like "sit," "stay," and "come." Short, fun sessions of 5–10 minutes work best. Early socialization alongside training greatly improves long-term behavior.
