# When is a dog considered a senior?

Dogs are generally considered senior at 7 years of age for medium and large breeds. Small breeds may not reach senior status until 10–12 years, while giant breeds like Great Danes may be considered seniors at just 5–6 years. Larger dogs age faster due to the physiological demands of their size.

# What health issues are common in senior dogs?

Common health issues in older dogs include arthritis, dental disease, cognitive dysfunction syndrome (canine dementia), heart disease, kidney disease, vision and hearing loss, and cancer. Regular biannual vet visits help detect these conditions early when they are most treatable.

# How should I adjust my senior dog's diet?

Senior dogs often benefit from food with higher protein to maintain muscle mass, lower calories to prevent weight gain, and added supplements like omega-3 fatty acids for joint health and antioxidants for cognitive function. Consult your vet before switching — individual health conditions may require specific dietary adjustments.
