# What plants are toxic to dogs?

Common plants toxic to dogs include azaleas, rhododendrons, sago palms, tulip bulbs, daffodils, oleander, yew, and lily of the valley. Sago palms are especially dangerous and can cause liver failure from even small ingestions. Keep these plants out of reach or remove them from your home and yard entirely.

# How do I keep my dog safe in hot weather?

Never leave your dog in a parked car — temperatures can reach deadly levels within minutes. Provide shade and fresh water outdoors, avoid walking on hot asphalt (test with the back of your hand — if it's too hot for you, it's too hot for paws), and limit exercise to early morning or evening during heat waves.

# What should I do if my dog eats something toxic?

Call the ASPCA Animal Poison Control Center (888-426-4435) or your veterinarian immediately. Do not induce vomiting unless instructed by a professional. Have the product name, quantity ingested, and your dog's weight ready. Time is critical — acting within the first hour greatly improves outcomes for most poisonings.
