# What supplies do I need for a new dog?

Essential supplies for a new dog include a properly sized crate, collar and leash, ID tag and microchip, food and water bowls, age-appropriate food, a dog bed, toys for play and chewing, poop bags, a brush appropriate for the coat type, and nail clippers. A first aid kit designed for dogs is also recommended.

# What size crate does my dog need?

Your dog's crate should be large enough for them to stand up, turn around, and lie down comfortably — but not so large that they can use one end as a bathroom. Measure your dog's height (standing) and length (nose to tail), then add 4 inches to both dimensions when selecting a crate size.

# What type of leash is best for my dog?

A standard 4–6 foot nylon or leather leash is best for everyday walks and training. Retractable leashes offer less control and can be dangerous in traffic or around other animals. For dogs that pull, a front-clip harness or head halter provides better control than a standard collar while avoiding trachea strain.
