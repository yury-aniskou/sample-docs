# How much exercise does my dog need per day?

Most adult dogs need 30 minutes to 2 hours of exercise daily depending on breed, age, and health. High-energy breeds like Border Collies and Huskies need 1–2 hours, while lower-energy breeds like Bulldogs do well with 30 minutes. Puppies and senior dogs need gentler, shorter activity sessions.

# What are good exercises for dogs?

Great ways to exercise your dog include brisk walks, fetch, swimming, agility courses, tug-of-war, and hide-and-seek with toys. Mental stimulation through puzzle feeders and training sessions is equally important and can tire a dog as effectively as physical activity.

# Can I over-exercise my dog?

Yes, over-exercising can be harmful, especially for puppies whose growth plates haven't closed (typically before 12–18 months), brachycephalic breeds (flat-faced dogs like Pugs), and senior or arthritic dogs. Signs of over-exercise include excessive panting, limping, refusing to move, and soreness the next day.
