# What should I look for on a dog food label?

Look for a named protein source (e.g., "chicken" not "poultry by-products") as the first ingredient, an AAFCO nutritional adequacy statement, and a feeding guideline by weight. Avoid foods with excessive fillers like corn syrup, artificial preservatives (BHA, BHT), and unnamed meat meals.

# Is grain-free dog food better for my dog?

Grain-free diets are not inherently healthier for most dogs. The FDA has investigated a potential link between grain-free diets high in legumes and a heart condition called dilated cardiomyopathy (DCM). Unless your dog has a confirmed grain allergy, a balanced diet with quality grains is generally safe and nutritious.

# How do I know if my dog is at a healthy weight?

A healthy dog has a visible waist when viewed from above, ribs that are easily felt but not prominently visible, and a slight abdominal tuck. Your vet can confirm using a Body Condition Score (BCS) scale from 1–9, with 4–5 being ideal. Obesity shortens a dog's lifespan and increases disease risk.
